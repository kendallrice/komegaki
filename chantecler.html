Chantecler

Kendall Rice

“I do declare,” said Chantecler

One morning to his lady fair,

“Were I a horse, and you my mare,

We shouldn’t make a finer pair!”

The lady thought, “You silly sot,

Who crows his love without a thought

And shall, if he don’t hush, be got

Into the farmer’s kitchen pot!”

The rooster read inside her head

And didn’t hush, but crowed instead

The louder: “Love, I’d fain be dead

Than let my loving go unsaid!”

The farmer heard his every word

And got the gist (for he spoke Bird),

And from his bed the farmer stirred—

But Chantecler was undeterred.

“For you, dear hen, I’d leave our pen

And stroll into the fox’s den

For all the pleasures in my ken

On you, my darling, do depen’.”

“Oh, aren’t you nice! And what device

Will fetch you out, you cockatrice?

Perhaps your ogling may suffice

To freeze that fox as hard as ice?”

“My loving gaze,” he turned the phrase,

“Is but one symptom of the craze

You cast on me; with warming rays

You melt my heart to hollandaise.”

’Twas not his charm, but threat of harm

That set her squawking in alarm—

For here’s the master of the farm,

A cauldron dangling from his arm!

“To hear you whine would be just fine

If I’d the sleep that’s rightly mine;

The moon’s still up, the stars still shine,

But up I am, so—time to dine.”

The farmer came, and lit a flame,

And at our birds the pot did aim—

Therein, corn kernels spelled the name

Of chantecler, and words: je t’aime.

“Enjoy, you two,” he said, and threw

A pair of spoons into the brew:

“And in return, no crows from you

Today—I’m sleeping in till two!”

The threat diffused, the fowls bemused,

The farmer scampered home and snoozed;

“Let’s eat, I guess!” the hen enthused;

The spoons, of course, went quite unused.

Till dawn they ate, and slumbered late

(Their bellies in so stuffed a state),

And shared a dream about a fate

They might have met atop a plate.

The boy-yolk sighed, “My love, we’re fried;

The fact cannot be much denied.”

To which the girl-yolk cooed, “Abide,

For even here, I’m by your side.”

We too will chart a course and start

Our life’s grand voyage, ne’er to part;

So please accept, with all my heart,

This humble piece of chicken-art.
