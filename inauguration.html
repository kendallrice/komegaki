The Inauguration

Kendall Rice

We’re welcoming the Turnip King

Who just got voted in last spring

So he’ll relax the turnip tax

(One can’t squeeze blood from everything);

He’s all alone on his new throne,

Whence prior rulers’ roots had grown,

The soil to suck till each we’d pluck

(It wasn’t theirs; ’twas just on loan).

The office passed, we lunch at last

On all the humus he’s amassed;

His Majesty’s new policies

Will thank us for the votes we cast:

Our patch he’s vowed to have de-cowed—

No heifers, calves, or bulls allowed—

Our last six kings vowed lots of things,

But this one just might do us proud.

At half-past three, the king will flee

Beneath the earth, where he is free,

And disappear for one more year

Of worthless vegetocracy.
