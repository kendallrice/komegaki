Square Peg, Round Hole

Kendall Rice

Once upon a time in the Latin language,

Poets dipped their pens in an eastern meter

Made by Greeks for Greeks and, instead of failing,

Somehow got famous.

Watch me try to do the same thing in English.

Don’t you think me brave for ignoring nature

And imposing beats where your ears don’t want them,

Brave like Catullus?

It would likely help if I had a mistress

Coy like Marvell’s (although my wife would kill me);

You’d respect my pining in lines ill-fitting—

I’d be immortal!

Now my brief flirtation with Sapphic meter

Lurches to an end, later than it should have

By about three stanzas. My poor beleaguered

English, I free you!
